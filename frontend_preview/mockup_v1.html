Rama is the central hero of the Ramayana, described as the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu. He is the eldest son of King Dasaratha of Ayodhya and Queen Kausalya.

The sources describe Rama as:

"Full of fire, indomitable, victorious, powerful, intelligent and wise... the right hand and very life's breath of his father."

His significance extends beyond being a warrior prince. He represents dharma (righteousness) personified - a model of virtue, duty, and moral conduct. His exile to the forest, the abduction of his wife Sita by Ravana, and the subsequent war to rescue her form the epic narrative.

Rama is also known by many names including Ramachandra, Raghava, and Raghunandan, reflecting his lineage from the Raghu dynasty.
